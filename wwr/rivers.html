Explore Our Rivers

Frogg Whitewater Rafting offers adventures on some of the most exciting and scenic rivers. Each river offers a unique experience from beginner-friendly waters to challenging rapids for the seasoned adventurer.

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return, the Salmon River was given the nickname "River of No Return" by locals because of its captivating beauty. It's common to see herds of elk, deer, and bighorn sheep, along with towering ponderosa pines that provide shade for campsites. Rapids range from Class I-IV, making it suitable for all experience levels. Early summer brings higher, colder water, while late summer offers warmer, slower flows, ideal for families.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Experience the remote beauty of the Green River as it winds through Desolation and Gray Canyons in central Utah. This 84-mile trip features high stone cliffs, desert landscapes, and a mix of Class II/III rapids. The route from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid is a journey through history, with prehistoric sites and stunning geological formations. Designated a National Landmark, Desolation Canyon offers an unforgettable 6-day rafting adventure.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

Rafting through the Grand Canyon is a bucket-list adventure combining world-class whitewater with awe-inspiring scenery. This iconic river journey takes you through thrilling rapids rated I-X, unique to the Grand Canyon. Along the way, you'll discover ancient ruins and side canyons accessible only by river, offering a deep dive into the canyon's rich history.
